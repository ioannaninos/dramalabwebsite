Welcome to THE DRAMALABS! Thank you for signing up to direct a play! Use this website as a guide for you and your cast as you prepare for your show date. As always, reach out to the Producers if you have any questions or concerns!

Step 1: Find a script!

You can't direct a play without a script to work with! Take a look at the scripts that the Producers sent you, and decide which one speaks the most to you. Choose one that excites you that you would be willing to spend a few weeks working on.

Step 2: Reach out!

Reach out to your cast via email introducing yourself and set up a quick meeting to get to know them and talk about a regular rehearsal time that works for everyone.

Step 3: Do a read-through!

Schedule your first rehearsal with the cast. This can be on the casual side; you can sit down together and do a full read through of the script. After, discuss it to make sure everyone has a solid understanding of the play before moving onto memorizing lines. Before leaving, make sure you have the next few rehearsals scheduled.

Step 4: Rehearse, rehearse, rehearse!

Meet regularly as a group to stage your play! When blocking a show, guide your actors by giving them specific locations and movements to do, but also give them the freedom to make their own choices for their character.

Step 5: Get technical!

As you rehearse, make a list of what your actors will need beyond their bodies to make the show happen. Costumes, props, a set, etc, these are all crucial to The DramaLabs!

Step 6: Tech Thursday!

The Thursday evening before your performance, the Producers will schedule a rehearsal in the classroom with you and the other plays to go over tech. Come prepared with any sound cues or music that you might need, and have some ideas for the lights so that the Producers can easily program the cues and have them ready for the show. You will then do a full runthrough of your DramaLab with lights and sound to get an idea of what the real thing will look like!

Step 7: Break a leg!

Invite all your friends and meet in the theatre classroom on Friday night to watch the big performance! Relish in the joy and glory of a show gone well (and even if it something went wrong, it was still a fun time, so don't stress!). Sign up to direct or perform in another DramaLab so that we can see you again soon!
